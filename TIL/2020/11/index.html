November 1st

Today I learned the definition of the Krull topology.

November 2nd

Today I learned about the regulator of a number field.

November 3rd

Today I learned an interesting example of a field extension, namely $\QQ(\zeta_p)/\QQ(\zeta_p+\zeta_p^{-1}).$

November 4th

Today I learned the asymptotic formula for derangements.

November 5th

Today I learned the law of quadratic reciprocity in function fields.

November 6th

Today I learned a proof of the Basel problem from Fourier analysis.

November 7th

Today I learned an explicit example where the naive approach of multiplying integral bases in a field composite does not necessarily yield an integral basis.

November 8th

Today I learned that it's possible to do calculus over ratios of prime numbers, from \href{https://mathoverflow.net/questions/311085/riemann-sum-formula-for-definite-integral-using-prime-numbers}{here}.

November 9th

Today I learned that the outline for the proof that, in an extension of number fields $L/K,$ if for every unramified prime $\mf p$ we have that $f(\mf q/\mf p)$ is constant for all primes $\mf q$ over $\mf p,$ then $L/K$ is actually Galois.

November 10th

Today I learned some perspectives on those $\arctan$ facts that pop every once in a while.

November 11th

Today I learned the proof that splitting of $\mf p$ in a non-Galois extension $L/K$ is in bijection with cosets $H\sigma D_\mf P$ for some prime $\mf P$ over $\mf p$ in the Galois closure $M$ of $L/K,$ where $H$ is subgroup of $G=\op{Gal}(M/K)$ fixing $L,$ and $D_\mf P$ is the decomposition subgroup.

November 12th

Today I learned the classification of nilpotent matrices with maximal index.

November 13th

Today I learned that the inverse Galois problem holds for abelian groups, and it's actually fairly nice.

November 14th

Today I learned a proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, using Galois theory as a translator.

November 15th

Today I learned about parameterizing rationals on conics.

November 16th

Today I learned about discrete valuation rings.

November 17th

Today I learned the definition of the limit superior to prove the radius of convergence for a power series.

November 18th

Today I learned the estimate for the number of ideals of bounded norm in imaginary quadratic fields.

November 19th

Today I learned the group structure of the elements of norm $1$ in $\QQ(i),$ and some semblance of generalizations to number fields.

November 20th

Today I learned that the group $\QQ/\ZZ$ has every finite cyclic group as a unique subgroup, and in fact no subgroup of it suffices.

November 21st

Today I learned a nice perspective on completely multiplicative functions on $\ZZ^+\to\ZZ^+,$ from HMMT Guts.

November 22nd

Today I learned the finish for the fact that the greedy algorithm for Egyptian fractions works.

November 23rd

Today I learned the group structure for the rational points with prime-power denominator on the unit hyperbola $x^2-y^2=1.$

November 24th

Today I learned the group structure for rational points on the unit hyperbola.

November 25th

Today I learned the reflection formula for the gamma function.

November 26th

Today I learned the definition of a cokernel, from Vakil.

November 27th

Today I learned the definition of homology groups from algebraic topology, from the Napkin.

November 28th

Today I learned a proof that the only automorphism of $\RR$ is the identity.

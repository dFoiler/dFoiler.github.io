October 1st

Today I learned the next lemma in the classification of finite abelian groups.

October 2nd

Today I learned the definition of the semidirect product.

October 3rd

Today I learned the finish of the proof in the classification of finite abelian groups.

October 4th

Today I learned an interesting application of M\"obius inversion.

October 5th

Today I learned about some characters of $(\QQ,+),$ as always from Keith Conrad.

October 6th

Today I learned a loose connection between Fourier analysis and theory of Dirichlet series and convolutions.

October 7th

Today I learned about adeles $\AA_\QQ$ in order to construct the characters over $(\QQ,+).$

October 8th

Today I learned a nice argument that if all roots of a polynomial $P(x)\in\ZZ[x]$ have magnitude no more than $1,$ then they are all either $0$ or roots of unity.

October 9th

Today I learned that every character $\chi:\QQ\to S^1$ can indeed by expressed as $\Psi_a$ for some adele $a\in\AA_\QQ.$

October 10th

Today I learned the finale to the classification of $\widehat\QQ.$

October 11th

Today I learned that the commutator subgroup (subgroup generated by $aba^{-1}b^{-1}$) of $S_n$ is $A_n,$ for $n\ge3.$

October 12th

Today I learned that the leading digits of Fibonacci numbers $F_n$ follow Benford's Law.

October 13th

Today I learned that the connection between $\left(\frac\bullet p\right)$ and quadratic forms doesn't need quadratic reciprocity.

October 14th

Today I learned that for a number field $K,$ there exists an extension $L/K$ such that every ideal $I\subseteq\mathcal O_K$ has $I\mathcal O_L$ principal (!), from \href{https://math.stackexchange.com/questions/294150/motivation-behind-the-definition-of-ideal-class-group?rq=1}{this post}.

October 15th

Today I learned the proof for the existence of non-Euclidean principal ideal domains.

October 16th

Today I learned the proof for the lower bound that comparison sorts must take at least $\Omega(n\log n)$ time.

October 17th

Today I learned a little bit about Eisenstein's criterion, in hopes of finding a polynomial for which Eisenstein's criterion fails for any transformation.

October 18th

Today I learned how to generate an irreducible polynomial which can't be proven with the standard tricks behind Eisenstein's Criterion.

October 19th

Today I learned about \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midy's_theorem}{Midy's Theorem}.

October 20th

Today I learned a proof for a slightly weaker version of Cohn's irreducibility criterion from \href{https://mast.queensu.ca/~murty/murty.pdf}{Murty}.

October 21st

Today I learned the correct context for the statement last week that if $P(x)\in\ZZ[x]$ only has roots which have magnitude less than or equal to $1,$ then all of those roots are roots of unity.

October 22nd

Today I learned that the trace pairing over finite fields gives all possible $\FF_q$-linear maps $\FF_{q^r}\to\FF_q,$ from \href{https://sites.math.rutgers.edu/~sk1233/courses/finitefields-F13/intro.pdf}{here}.

October 23rd

Today I learned a reason that monogenic extensions are, in a sense, kind of rare.

October 24th

Today I learned the proof of Wedderburn's Theorem showing that finite domains $D$ are all fields.

October 25th

Today I learned the definition of the categorical kernel.

October 26th

Today I learned an elementary proof that the set of primes which split completely in a Galois extension $K/\QQ$ of degree $n$ have density $1/n,$ only assuming some minor analytic facts about the Dedekind zeta function.

October 27th

Today I learned a proof (after having skimmed one a few days ago) that a Galois extension is completely determined by its completely split primes.

October 28th

Today I learned an equivalent (Vakil's) definition of a kernel in a category with $0$ objects.

October 29th

Today I learned the spigot algorithm for hexadecimal digits of $\pi.$

October 30th

Today I learned a cute but unsurprising connection in some infinite Galois theory.

October 31st

Today I learned the definition of Pr\"ufer groups.
